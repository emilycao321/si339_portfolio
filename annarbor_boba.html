Ann Arbor Bobas

I've been to practically all of the Ann Arbor boba stores/chain and each one of them has their pros and cons. I will be listing it out here my favorite bubble tea drink and what are the pros and cons about each store.

Description:

Boba Tea House provides fresh and delicious bubble tea and other drinks. Their goal is to not only give the highest-qaulity drinks but also a new sensation while drinking bubble tea. There's a wide-variety of bubble tea menus with many flavors and they offer other dirnks, including fruit tea, fruit juice, coffee, yogurt, and many more to accomodate other customer's taste. They try to serve healthy drinks to support their customer's healthy lifestyle.

Chatime

Description:

With their slogan, "Shaking Tea Up ," Chatime really do shake things up. They have been around since 2005, serving millions of consumers with their favorite cup of tea, making it part of every occasion, celerbation or simply cupping a busy day at work. They are the largest bubble tea brand eyes US expansion. The word “Cha” means tea in Taiwan. So technically ‘chatime’ could be loosely translated into ‘teatime’. Finest teas from Taiwan and unique flavour of bubble tea bring new drinks tea style for good user experience in the whole world.

CoCo Fresh Tea & Juice

Description:

CoCo Fresh Tea & Juice is a global bubble tea brand with over 3,000 stores worldwide. They started in Taiwan with its headquarter located in Tamsui since 1997. They have currently have extensive footprints throughout Asia, South Pacific and North America. CoCo introduces its all-time favorite bubble tea to New York in the Winter of 2011, opening its first store across the street from Baruch College. In addition to a wide variety of bubble tea selections and healthy fruit tea options, CoCo Fresh Tea & Juice is dedicated to recreating tea drinking experience, introducing new drinks to the market.

Ding Tea

Description:

As Ding Tea's slogan goes, "Shake for Life", their drinks are shaking up the consumer's life with high-quality drinks. Since 2007, the founder has intended to promote the brand and Taiwanese tea culture to the world, created a new mainstream of exclusive drinks, and insisted on bringing consumers new and refined drinks. They keep a close eye on our quality and never use overnight tea. Also, they emphasize freshly shaking tea and SOP standard to maintain same quality of each cup of tea. With blending fresh tea + strict tea selection + exclusive and unique taste, they care about every detail and adhere to the professional quality and perfectness.

Quickly

Description:

Quickly was founded in California in 1996. They marketed themselves as a New Generation Asian Fusion-style cafe in the United States. They have successfully broadened its unique branding image from Taiwan throughout the world. They became one of the largest tapioca milk tea franchises in the world, with over 2000 locations in Africa, Asia, Europe and North America. Along with selling a variety of drinks, Quickly also sells numerous of snacks, ranging from popcorn chicken to egg puffs.

Sharetea

Description:

Sharetea was established in 1992 and they started off their business by selling to-go black tea and pearl milk tea in Taipei, Taiwan. Since then Sharetea has opened many branches and franchises in 16 countries such as United States, Canada, Southeast Asia, Australia, Dubai and over 300 stores. They ensure all their products are 100% guaranteed to arrive fresh and tasty. They test the tea leaves each growing season and all ingredients from time to time to ensure their drinks are consistent and are of their best quality. The ingredients and high quality tea leaves are shipped directly from Taiwan.

Sweeting

Description:

At Ann Arbor, there are two Sweeting locations: one on campus and the other in Briarwood Mall. They were previously named MomoTea, then to Sweetology, but later changed to Sweeting due to legal notice from a company in St. Louis who is already using the name. MomoTea established in Auckland New Zealand in 2001. The MomoTea store, but later changed to Sweeting, opened in 2009. Sweeting is a Chinese store that opened in 2015 at the South University location. Other than milk tea, Sweeting sells rolled ice cream. Rolled iced cream originated from Thailand. The fun and creativity that comes from the rolled ice cream became the reason Sweeting sells them.

TeaNinja is not only providing plenty of Vegan choices, but also creating a series of original beverages rich in tea rhyme, fruity aroma and creamy milk. They believe that every kind of raw material is full of the beauty of heaven and earth and human understanding of nature.
