The source of all bubbles teas it would seem, at Chun Shui Tang teahouse in Taichung, Taiwan. The founder, Liu Han-Chieh, first came up with the idea of serving Chinese tea cold in the early 1980s after visiting Japan where he saw coffee served cold. Then, in 1988, his product development manager, Ms. Lin Hsiu Hui, was sitting in a staff meeting and had brought with her a typical Taiwanese dessert called fen yuan, a sweetened tapioca pudding. Just for fun she poured the tapioca balls into her Assam iced tea and drank it. This was how bubble milk tea came about.


Most bubble milk tea chains originated from Taiwan, but bubble milk tea is part of a lot of Asian cultures. In Asian cultures, tea is a big part of our tradition. We drink tea when we are eating out with families and friends or when giving our offerings to our elderlies or ancestors. I am one of the people who are deeply in love with bubble milk tea. As long as I have a variety of flavor of milk tea, I can drink them every day. For health reasons and my hope to live a long life, I limit myself from drinking too much milk teas, especially those that have bobas in them. I will either limit myself or if I really want milk tea, I will opt out the bobas. The bobas aren't harmful when eaten moderately, but if too much of them are in the human system, the body won't react well to it.
