New York City Bobas

Growing up in New York City as an Asian/American, I have explored and tasted a variety of bubble tea from different places. It was one of the many ways my friends and I hang out with one another. In this list, I will be putting my favorite tea/bubble tea stores that weren't already listed in the Ann Arbor section. I haven't tried all the bubble tea stores in New York City, but below are my favorite stores.


Bubbleology

Description:

Bubbleology, a U.K.-based beverage leader specializing in authentic bubble tea, was founded in 2011 in the heart of trendsetting Soho, London. Today, they are a leading bubble tea brand, with a rapid expansion both in the United Kingdom and the USA. They are not just a tea brand, they are a vibrant lifestyle proposition with key partnerships among major retailers and other exciting and popular fashion brands. Their menu is constantly evolving, with new product innovations a regular feature of the Bubbleology brand. They are continually thinking outside the box, pushing boundaries and developing innovative and on-trend new product launches.

Location:

120 1/2 1st Ave, New York, NY 10009

Rating

4.3 (300 Google reviews)

My Top 3 Faves

  • Bobatella
  • Assam Milk Tea

Gong Cha

Description:

Known for quality tea and service, Gong Cha has withheld its reputation against many existing competitors. Gong Cha was founded in 2006 in Kaohsiung, Taiwan. Gong Cha translates to “tribute tea for the emperor” opened its doors into a highly saturated bubble tea market. Since its establishment in 2006, Gong Cha has expanded to over 15 countries and 1500+ locations worldwide and growing. Gong Cha offers premium quality tea, products and services, such as fresh tea brewed and pearl (bubbles) cooked to perfection every 4 hours, tea sourced from the finest Taiwan tea estates, and strict quality control for all ingredients. Gong Cha USA specialty known as “Milk Foam” became a hit with many customers as it offered a new take on tea-based drinks. Their core values of quality, innovation and service have made Gong Cha the successful bubble tea brand it is today.

Location:

72 Bayard St, New York, NY 10013

Rating

4.5 (272 Google reviews)

My Top 3 Faves

  • Oreo Milk Tea
  • Earl Grey Milk Tea
  • Matcha Milk Tea with Red Bean

Kung Fu Tea

Description:

Kung Fu, in its purest essence, is the desire for self-improvement and to expand one's capabilities beyond all limitations. Kung Fu Tea's mission to not only continuously improve our brand and beverages and the lives of our customers through providing only the most delicious, high-quality flavors but also to inspire the community to dream big and live fearlessly. It was founded in Queens, NY on April 30, 2010. They are America’s largest bubble tea brand with over 250+ locations across the U.S. Their milk tea is made with the art of ‘3T’ Kung Fu, every cup of tea is freshly brewed at the perfect temperature using premium tea leaves. On April 30, 2018 (their 8th birthday), they founded National Bubble Tea Day to honor the growing community of people who live and breathe bubble tea while celebrating the beverage that has made our lives bubblier.

Tiger Sugar

Description:

Tiger Sugar is the creator of the original brown sugar bubble tea with fresh cream and a unique tiger stripe design. They have grown into an undisputed cult brand of Boba worldwide. They were founded in Taichung, Taiwan, in 2017, as the Birth of a Tiger. Their drinks not only taste good but tell their unique story with "tiger stripes" that are infused onto all of their drinks with unique hand-poured syrups. While many company have tried to replicate their signature brown sugar boba milk tea, but due to their tried and proven methods stand the test of time. They use a proprietary 8-hour method for cooking their syrups and tapioca pearls. This results in the perfect marriage of tea, milk, and syrup in every sip. Their brand is bold, and their unforgettable drink varieties are a testament to this.

Location:

197 Canal St, New York, NY 10013

Rating

4.4 (533 Google reviews)

ViVi Bubble Tea

Description:

Vivi was founded in 2007 in New York City and became a franchise in 2012 with a chain of over 70 branches. This expansion, with their goal of spreading the footsteps of traditional bubble tea to all parts of the country, demonstrates their first step in the undertaking of such a mission. They guarantee to provide customers the best of quality and service, strictly only using ingredients shipped directly from Taiwan, all of which approved by FDA, to brew you the freshest and most authentic Taiwanese taste in every sip. The shop offers a full drink and food menu. They provide a variety of teas such as milk teas, flavored teas, fresh fruit teas and floral teas as well as fresh milk lattes and slushes. Their food menu consists of their signature popcorn chicken as well as bentos, ramen and curry.

Yaya Tea

Description:

Yaya Tea is a café specializing in freshly brewed tea through a purifier filtration system where premium tea leaves are brewed fresh daily. They take bubble tea to another level by adding various jellies or popping boba into their drinks that burst in the your mouth. They serve unique imported snacks as well, mostly from Japan! They stand out amongst our competitors by serving handmade Onigiri, also known as a Riceball, which are only made to order. Onigiri is a popular Japanese snack that is convenient, handheld and delicious! It is served with our special Nori wrapping, also known as dried seaweed, which stays separate from the Riceball to remain crispy until it is ready to be eaten!

Location:

51 Chrystie St, New York, NY 10002

Rating
